What is Blood Donation?

Blood donation is a voluntary process where a healthy person gives blood to help patients in medical emergencies. Donated blood is used during surgeries, accident cases, cancer treatments, organ transplants, and childbirth complications. One unit of blood can save up to three lives because it is separated into red cells, plasma, and platelets.

Blood Donation Process

The donation process begins with registration and basic details. A mini health check-up including blood pressure, temperature, and hemoglobin level is conducted. Blood collection takes about 8–10 minutes. After donation, donors rest briefly and are provided refreshments. The entire procedure is safe and completed within 30–45 minutes.

Types of Blood Donation

There are different types of blood donation including Whole Blood Donation, Platelet Donation, Plasma Donation, and Double Red Cell Donation. Whole blood donation is the most common type. The collected blood is separated into components to help multiple patients with different needs.

Eligibility Criteria

A donor must be between 18 and 65 years of age, weigh at least 50 kg, and be in good general health. Individuals should not have recent infections or major surgeries. Healthy individuals can donate blood once every three months without any risk.

Benefits of Donating Blood

Blood donation helps reduce harmful iron levels in the body, improves cardiovascular health, and provides a free mini health screening. Most importantly, it offers emotional satisfaction knowing that you have contributed to saving lives and helping families in need.
